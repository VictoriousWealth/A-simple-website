About the Staff/Student Liaison Committee (SSLCom)

SSLCom is a bridge of communication between students and staff, in matters such as teaching, examinations, departmental policies and student issues. Student reps can raise the views of students to the department, regarding:

  • Student feedback on the quality of teaching;
  • Inputs to the planning of curriculum changes;
  • Departmental services (e.g. hand-in arrangements, office opening times, study facilities, the departmental network, availability of personal tutors etc.);
  • Improving channels of communication between staff and students students.

If you have any feedback about any aspect of your course, the university, or issues relevant to student life that you would like to take to a SSLCom meeting, you can contact one of your student reps - details will be listed here once recruited.

Academic Reps are encouraged to meet ahead of SSLCom to decide:

  • Which reps will attend SSLCom, if not all
  • What points they plan to raise at SSLCom

In summary, the SSLCom is made up of academic reps that speak on behalf of other students.

But how do you get hold of the reps?

You can email all reps collectively on com-student-reps -group@sheffield.ac.uk or you can contact individual reps for your year group. You can find more info here. Or if you fancy, you could become an academic rep! Yes, it's possible go and read the next section to know more about this fantastic opportunity!

Becoming an Academic Rep

Academic Rep Recruitment is open for everyone! Becoming an Academic Rep is a perfect way to get more involved in making changes, whether that's in your Department, through the Union, or in the wider world. Together with student leaders and support from your Department, you'll take on a role where you can find your passion and reimagine what education looks like at Sheffield, your unfiversity.
